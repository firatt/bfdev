Computational Bloodflow Dynamics

Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) is a well-established tool used in engineering for long time. Bioengineers have adopted CFD to study complex physiological flows in fields such as cardiovascular medicine. We are using CFD-based techniques to build complex computer representations of the cardiovascular system in health and disease. Our aim is to use CFD for enhancing diagnostic assessment, to predict physiological responses to intervention and compute previously unmeasurable hemodynamic parameters.

Soft Tissue Engineering

Congenital heart defects (CHD) are structural problems arising from abnormal formation of heart or major blood vessels, and the most common cause of infant death from birth defects. We are interested to understand the mechanism of CHD, particularly caused by abnormal remodeling of blood vessels, and its relation with mechanical factors. We looked at the relation between hemodynamics, growth and gene expression in right vitelline artery and aortic arches of native chick embryos (Goktas et al. 2016, Karakaya et al. 2018). Moreover, we perform alterations in mechanical loading conditions and genetics coupled with quantification and imaging analyses to focus on the disease progression and possible cures.


Cardiovascular Biology

In cardiovascular biology, a field in which we examine mechanisms of cardiovascular physiology and pathophysiology, we examine the structure and surfaces of different vessel samples including LIMA (Left Internal Mammary Artery), RIMA (Right Internal Mammary Artery), VSM (Vena Saphena Magna) and karotid artery obtained from various cardiac patients. In this study, SEM (Scanning Electron Microscopy) analysis of native arterial and venous grafts was performed in 54 patients with off-pump coronary artery bypass graft. The grafts used in the bypass operation and the patient risk groups were compared and the effect of thrombogenic involvement of 2 different solution concentrations used in graft preparation was also investigated. Moreover, we are working on the process of visualization of these vessel samples in TEM (Transmission Electron Microscope) for the investigation of intracellular structure and materials.


Cardiovascular Devices

Cardiovascular Devices are used to diagnose, monitor or treat heart disease and related health problems. These devices can be listed as implantable sensors, pacemakers, stents and, Ventricular assist devices (VADs). In our research group, we focused on VADs used in single ventricle congenital heart disease. A novel passive device comprising an aortic turbine coupled with a venous pump, namely iATVA has been designed and tested[1] as a potential treatment of Fontan patients. Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) optimization of iATVA is currently ongoing. Also, we currently work on novel polymeric artificial heart valves for Right Ventricular Outflow Tract Reconstruction (RVOT), performance optimization of these devices and novel manufacturing/implantation methods.
